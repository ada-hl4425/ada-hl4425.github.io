Research Interests

I'm fascinated by the intersection of physical sciences, computational methods, and machine learning. My research focuses on developing and applying computational models to understand complex environmental, chemical and physical systems.

Atmospheric & Environmental Modeling

Chemical transport modeling, atmospheric chemistry simulations, and climate-related risk assessment using both physics-based and data-driven approaches.

Physics-Informed Machine Learning

Integrating physical laws with neural networks for solving PDEs, surrogate modeling, and scientific computing applications where data is sparse but physics is known.

Uncertainty Quantification

Bayesian methods, Monte Carlo simulations, and probabilistic modeling to characterize and propagate uncertainties in complex environmental systems.

Computational Geoscience

Geospatial analysis, geochemical modeling, and applying computational methods to understand Earth system processes and hazard assessment.

Research Philosophy: I believe the future of environmental science lies in hybrid approaches that combine mechanistic understanding with modern data science. By encoding physical principles into machine learning frameworks, we can build models that are both data-efficient and physically consistent—essential for tackling pressing challenges in climate science and sustainability.
